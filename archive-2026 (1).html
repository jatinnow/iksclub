Invited Talks

Distinguished Speakers

Ten leading scholars in Indian Knowledge Systems who delivered invited lectures at NCIKSST 2026.

Prof. M. D. Srinivas

Chairman, Centre for Policy Studies, Chennai  ·  Padma Shri 2025

Seminal Contributions of the Kerala School of Mathematics and Astronomy

The Kerala School, founded by Mādhava in the 14th century, developed calculus and non-geocentric planetary models centuries before European counterparts. The talk covered Mādhava's infinite series for π, the Yuktibhāṣā proof, and Nīlakaṇṭha's quasi-heliocentric model of 1500 CE.

Prof. Amartya Kumar Dutta

Professor of Mathematics, ISI Kolkata  ·  INSA Teachers Award

Mathematics in Ancient India: Glimpses

A survey of landmark contributions from ancient Indian mathematics — from Vedic-period arithmetic to algebraic geometry — deserving wider recognition among modern academicians and intellectuals.

Prof. Anil Kumar Gourishetty

Professor of Physics & Head, Centre for IKS, IIT Roorkee

Promoting Sanskrit and IKS

Overview of platforms for promoting Sanskrit and IKS — including experiments at IIT Roorkee — and a discussion of challenges and opportunities for integrating STEM within IKS scholarship.

Prof. M. S. Sriram

Physicist & Historian, Prof. K. V. Sarma Research Foundation, Chennai  ·  Fellow INSA

Notable Features of Siddhāntic Astronomy

A survey from the Vedāṅga Jyotiṣa (c.1250 BC) through the Āryabhaṭīya (499 CE) to Bhāskarācārya's innovations — covering Indian trigonometry, instantaneous velocities (proto-calculus), and Nīlakaṇṭha's quasi-heliocentric model (1500 CE).

Prof. V. Ramanathan

Professor of Chemistry, IIT (BHU) Varanasi  ·  Adani Indology Fellow 2025

Insights from Spectroscopic Characterization of Hiṅgula & Haritāla

Haritāla (arsenic trisulphide) and Hiṅgula (mercuric sulphide) from Rasaśāstra examined via modern spectroscopic techniques — revealing how ancient taboos and constraints on their therapeutic use align with contemporary analytical findings.

Dr. Ketu Ramachandrasekhar

Director–Knowledge Products, Siddhanta Knowledge Foundation  ·  Ministry of Culture Fellow

Temple Architecture: Sacred Geometry, Consciousness, and Technology

Temples as living technologies — examining how ritual, sacred geometry, spatial proportion, and energy design integrate to create transformative environments aligned with cosmic and metaphysical principles.

Prof. Sunita T. Pandey

Professor of Agronomy, GBPUAT Pantnagar  ·  Rishi Parashar Award 2024

Vrikshayurveda: A Glorious Agricultural Heritage of Bharat and Its Scientific Validation, Herbal Kunap Jal in particular

Vrikshāyurveda-based agricultural technologies — with a focus on Herbal Kunapajala, the 1000-year-old fermented bio-fertilizer — and scientific validation results from tea gardens, wheat crops, and oilseeds across India.

Shri Raghava Krishna

Founder & CEO, Brhat Culture Creatives & Brhat Educational Trust

Cultural Strategies for Institutionalizing IKS Sciences

Examination of cultural phenomena and global megatrends influencing Indian imagination — and strategies for institutionalizing IKS sciences within national educational and policy frameworks.

Prof. Ramakrishna G. Bhat

Professor, Dept. of Chemistry, IISER Pune  ·  Conference Convenor

Metals and Medicines in Ancient Indian Chemistry: Teaching at its Best

Rasaśāstra — the ancient Indian science of chemistry and metallurgy — examined from the Vedic period through medieval India, with a focus on Bhasmā preparation and strategies for teaching IKS through modern scientific methods.

Prof. Alex Hankey

Professor Emeritus, MIT World Peace University, Pune  ·  Cambridge & MIT PhD

The Four Levels of Speech in Shiksha, the Vedic Science of Education

The Taittriya Upanishad's four levels of speech — Vaikere, Madhyama, Pashyanti, Para — examined through modern brain science, information theory, and quantum physics, revealing deep structural insights about human cognition.
